In more than 10 years of hosting our weekly Digital Lifestyle Show, we have seen many methods of storing and playing media (be that music, video, or photos) come and go.

At the start, most of our listeners stored their media, if they did so at all, on a Windows desktop PC and usually consumed it on the same PC. They might have had some sort of MP3 player, and iPods were beginning to make an impact, but it certainly wasn’t the diverse marketplace it is now.

We have seen the growth of both Apple’s iOS and Google’s Android platforms, the birth of downloadable media, and in recent years the surge of streaming services such as Netflix.

While in those early days we had a dream of one integrated system where we could listen to and view our media on our desktop, phone, and even games platform, this has dissolved into a world where media is usually tied to the technology family you most use, be it Microsoft, Apple, or Google.

Even within Microsoft’s platforms it has become increasingly difficult to find one solution to share your media.

This is why we are really excited by the opportunities Windows 10 brings. It has the promise of a universal Windows platform. This is a way of allowing apps to run on any variant of Windows 10 on any platform it supports. Windows 10 supports a lot of platforms from desktops and laptops to tablets and phones to even Xbox One and Raspberry Pi 2.

Microsoft has also cleverly made some of its core media apps from Windows 10 available on other platforms including Apple and Google.

Where your media comes from and how it is stored will still depend whether at heart you are a Microsoft, Apple, or Google devotee, but Windows 10 holds out the hope of being able to consume that media everywhere.

This book will help show you how to fulfill that ambition.

# 1. Music Services on the Go

If music be the food of love, play on, so said Duke Orsino in Shakespeare’s Twelfth Night. Our love of music is still as strong today as it was back then. However, we often can struggle to play our music where we want. Windows 10, coupled with the ability to use Microsoft’s music apps on Android and Apple devices, looks to solve that quandary. You can now take your music anywhere.

With Windows 10, you can enjoy music on your PC, laptop, tablet, or phone with a range of services.

In this chapter, you will look at how to download, stream, and purchase music on your Android, iOS, or Windows phone, tablet, or laptop with Microsoft Groove and third-party services. You will also find out how you can listen to radio stations and podcasts on the move.

## Listening to Your Music with Groove

Information

Microsoft changed the name of its music app in Windows 10 from Xbox Music, as it was known in Windows 8, to Groove. You may find that on some platforms with older versions it is still referred to as Xbox Music.

Groove is Microsoft’s music service that combines a number of features into one service that works with Windows 10, Xbox One, iPad, and Android devices.

With the services you can listen to your own music stored on a PC or phone, stream music from the cloud, and purchase music directly from your device.

Groove breaks down into four areas: listening to music stored on the device, streaming music from your personal collection on Microsoft’s servers, purchasing albums and songs from Microsoft, and using Microsoft’s music subscription service.

### Groove on a PC or Laptop

When you think of taking music on the move, you might be tempted to limit your thoughts to just phones or portable media players such as the iPod Touch. That would be wrong. Feedback from listeners to our weekly The Digital Lifestyle show reveals that a surprising amount of music playback on the go still occurs on Windows devices, such as the Surface line, and laptops. These will be running the desktop or PC version of Windows 10, and in this section you will look at what this offers for you to play your music.

Once you open the app, you are presented with a view of your collection of music stored on your PC (Figure [1-1](#A978-1-4842-1473-2_1_Chapter.html#Fig1)).

#### Playing Music

The music app can display your collection in different views including by album, artist, song, or playlist. You can pick your preferred view by selecting the relevant icon on the left side of the app. If the menu is not visible, you can use the menu button at the top left of the window to toggle it open.

Information

The menu button looks like three horizontal lines on top of each other. Some people think this looks like a hamburger, and thus this type of menu is often referred to as a hamburger menu (shown earlier in Figure [1-1](#A978-1-4842-1473-2_1_Chapter.html#Fig1)).

In the Artists view, the artists are sorted alphabetically, and selecting a letter brings up the alphabet where you can then jump to another letter, making it easy to quickly find your artists if you have a large collection. When you select an artist, the app shows the albums and song in your collection from those artists. You can either select Play, which is a rightward-pointing triangle, to play the songs from the artist or select the plus sign to add the songs to a playlist (more on that in a minute).

In the Albums view (Figures [1-4](#A978-1-4842-1473-2_1_Chapter.html#Fig4) and [1-5](#A978-1-4842-1473-2_1_Chapter.html#Fig5)), each album is represented by a thumbnail of the album cover. Clicking the cover shows the album details and track listing. Pressing the Play icon, a rightward-pointing triangle, will play whole album, or you can select an individual track by clicking it and pressing the Play button.

A playlist is a collection of songs that you can play back later. For example, you could create a list of songs for working out to or songs you like driving to. They can be any combination of music you like. To create a new playlist, you click/tap the plus icon in the icon list on the right side.

You can then enter a name for the playlist and click on Save. This creates a new playlist and then takes you back to your music collection.

When you click/tap a song, you will see a plus sign, which presents you with a list of your playlists; you can select the playlist you want to add the song to.

If you want to add a selection of songs, albums, or artists to a playlist or download a group of songs, there is an easy way to do it. Switch to the desired section by selecting either the Albums, Artists, or Songs view from the left menu. Then you can click/tap the Select button at the top.

#### Pinning Content to the Start Menu

Another great feature of Groove is the ability to pin artists, playlists, and songs to the Windows 10 Start menu. This makes it easy to start playing your favorite tracks directly from the Start menu.

To add an album, artist, or playlist to the Start menu, follow these steps:

Find what you want to add from the Groove app by clicking the album, artist, or playlist name.   Click the More button.   This will give you a pop-up menu where you will see the “Pin to start” option. Selecting this will add the album to the Start menu for easy access.  

You have now seen how to add music to your collection, play that music, and access it quickly. But what if you want to have access to music that is not currently in your collection? In the next few sections, you will learn how Groove in Windows 10 makes it easy to obtain music.

#### Purchasing Songs and Music Subscriptions

As well as Groove playing music stored on your tablet, PC, or the cloud, it also has a library of songs you can purchase. You can purchase a single song or an album. If you want unlimited access to music, there is something called a Groove Music Pass subscription.

##### Groove Music Pass

The Music Pass subscription is a service that you pay for monthly. With a subscription you can listen to or download any of the songs and albums available in Groove collections. You can browse the Groove Music collection in the Windows Store (as shown earlier in Figure [1-3](#A978-1-4842-1473-2_1_Chapter.html#Fig3)) for songs, artists, and albums and play them on your phone or tablet.

When you subscribe, you typically pay around $10 (£7) per month and can access the subscription from any of your devices. So, you can subscribe once and then listen to the music on your Android, iPhone, or Windows phone, tablet, and PC. If you buy a lot of music, then this is a service worth looking at. There is a 30-day free trial available also; you can find out more at [`https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/groove-music`](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/groove-music) .

Tip

In some countries, Microsoft’s store will often have bundle offers including Office 365 and a Groove Music Pass subscription at a discount. These can be worth looking out for.

##### Managing Your Devices

If you have a Groove Music Pass subscription, you can stream music on any of your Windows 10 devices as well as iOS, Android, and Xbox.

As you saw earlier in the chapter, you can also download music to play when you’re offline. However, there is a four-device limit with Groove, so you can download tracks only from four devices (including iOS, Android, and Windows devices).

The first time you download a song from a device, the device registers it with Groove, and each time you download a song on a new device, the device registers with Groove. Once you have four devices registered, you can’t download from another device; you can still stream music but not download it.

If you get rid of a phone or PC and want a new device to be registered in its place, you can remove a device from the list. You can do this by going to the Groove Devices page, which you can launch from Settings in the Groove app.

The Devices page (Figure [1-9](#A978-1-4842-1473-2_1_Chapter.html#Fig9)) lists the PC, phones, and tablets registered with Groove and the date they were registered.

![A978-1-4842-1473-2_1_Fig9_HTML.jpg](A978-1-4842-1473-2_1_Fig9_HTML.jpg)

Figure 1-9.

Managing your devices for Groove

You can click/tap the Remove button, and it will ask you to confirm your selection. When you do that, it will deregister the device, and you will still be able to stream music on it but not download music.

Note

You can remove only one device every 30 days.

#### Exploring Music

## How to Subscribe to Podcasts

Podcasts are Internet-based radio shows that you download and listen to on your phone, tablet, or PC. One example of this is The Digital Lifestyle show we record weekly.

The great thing about podcasts is that you subscribe to a show, and then when a producer creates a new episode, the podcast app you use will automatically download the episode to your device ready for you to listen to (or watch if it’s a video podcast) without you have to do anything.

With Windows 10 there are built-in tools for listening to podcasts as well as plenty of third-party apps you can use.

If the podcast you want is not showing in the search results, you can still subscribe by using the show’s RSS feed (RSS stands for Really Simple Syndication, and each podcast has a unique RSS feed link, for example [`http://thedigitallifestyle.com/podcast.xml`](http://thedigitallifestyle.com/podcast.xml) ).

This a special web page that podcast apps use to download shows. Generally each podcast producer will have a link to their RSS feed on their web site. To use the link with the phone podcast app, follow these steps:

This will paste the podcast feed into the search box.   Tap the search button, which will bring up the podcast information page. You can then click the “subscribe” button, play the podcast, or view the individual episodes.   Information

To avoid using up your mobile data allowance in the Podcasts app settings, you can set the app to download podcasts only over Wi-Fi.

# 2. Storing Your Music in the Cloud

Information

Just prior to the release of Windows 10, Microsoft rebranded its Xbox Music service as Groove. You may still see references to it by its old name.

In the previous chapter, you learned how to play music in Windows 10\. In this chapter, you will learn how you can store your music so that you can access it from just about anywhere. Also in this chapter, for a slight diversion, you will look at how you can consume your music on Xbox One.

Over the years playing your music on the move has been one of the biggest challenges facing enthusiasts. When portable music first started being available in electronic forms, you stored your music on your PC or Mac and then physically connected the device, perhaps an iPod or phone, that you wanted to transfer that music to.

This was always a slightly cumbersome and time-consuming process. It also relied on you knowing you wanted to play certain music on the go. People have always wanted to be able to access all their music at any time without having to plan for the event.

One of the really great features of Windows 10 on a PC is that any music you have stored on the device can be synced to the cloud for accessing from other Windows PCs and phones. In fact, as you saw in [Chapter 1](#A978-1-4842-1473-2_1_Chapter.html), Microsoft also has Groove Music apps for iOS and Android. So, you can have your music collection on your PC and instantly have it accessible from Windows, Android, and iOS phones and tablets.

You have a few different options for storing your music in the cloud. Let’s start by looking at Microsoft’s free solution.

Information

Storing music in the cloud can sound mystical, but all it really means is storing your data on a service that is accessible anywhere via the Internet. That service, be it owned by Microsoft, Google, or even Amazon, may have servers located all over the world, and your data may be stored on any one of them (although for privacy and efficiency reasons the server is likely to be physically close to your home location).

The point is you do not need to know where it is stored, only that it is accessible to you anywhere you are via the Internet.

## Using OneDrive to Store Your Music

OneDrive is Microsoft’s cloud storage solution that you can use to store any files, including documents, photos, and media files. You can then access your content via the Web and via the OneDrive apps Microsoft provides for iOS, Android, and Windows. Microsoft gives you 15GB of storage for free when you create a Microsoft account, and you can purchase more storage at OneDrive.com. If you have an Office 365 subscription, you automatically get 1TB of OneDrive storage.

The great thing about OneDrive and Groove Music is that the two services work together so that the music you store in OneDrive is available on your other devices.

When you first set up OneDrive on a Windows 10 PC, you get the option of syncing your entire OneDrive storage or selecting individual folder synchronization. If you have selected your entire collection, then you are ready to start, but if you haven’t or haven’t set up OneDrive yet, you need to tell OneDrive to synchronize your `Music` folder.

# 3. Watching Videos, Movies, and TV Shows

So far in this book, we have mainly concentrated on how to play music, but we know from our experience gathered from hosting our weekly podcast that many audience questions revolve around the world of playing video and its many confusing sources, formats, and containers. People often just do not get why a file they have downloaded from the Internet does not play.

Information

Sources are video streaming services such as Netflix, video hosting services such as YouTube, and media stored locally or on a network storage device.

Formats are the way the video is encoded for storage. These are often known as codecs (short for coder-decoder). Examples of codecs are MPEG, WMV, and MP4, but there are lots more.

Containers are the cause of a lot of confusion. These are basically a way of packaging up one or more media files for playback.

You could consider, for example, a DVD a container, and indeed a DVD can be converted to an ISO file for playback on a PC, but a DVD can contain many different media types including audio and video.

This is the same with other media containers including the popular MKV container. One issue is that software, including Windows 10, may claim to play back MKV files, but they will play back these containers only if they contain media in formats they understand.

Windows 10 has gone a long way to help this confusion, and we have found it much better at playing some of the more obscure file types. We have found most files we have downloaded just work. One exception is the playback of DVDs, which has been removed. However, Microsoft has now released a Windows DVD player app (available at [`https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/store/apps/windows-dvd-player/9nblggh2j19w`](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/store/apps/windows-dvd-player/9nblggh2j19w) for a fee). Third-party applications such as VLC, which we cover later in this chapter, can also help with this.

Windows 10 has also really helped control the potential cost of watching video streams, particularly when on metered connections such as a mobile phone network.

In this chapter, you will look at all the different ways you can use Windows 10 to watch videos from your own collection, from the Windows Store, from Netflix, and from YouTube.

You will also look at how other video apps including VLC can help you watch all your different video formats.

Finally, you will look how you can use Windows 10’s Data Sense app to monitor and control how much data you are using while streaming video.

# 5. Streaming Media Around the Home

With Windows 10 you can stream music and videos from media servers on your network using a standard called Digital Living Network Alliance (DLNA). DLNA is a certified standard for media servers, media players, and applications to work together. With DLNA you have a media server and something called a media renderer. The server could be a PC running Windows Media Player, a network-attached storage (NAS) box in your home, or media server software installed on a PC.

In the previous chapter, you learned how you can set up Windows Media Player to work as a media server, and in this chapter you will look at how you can stream from Windows Media Player and other media servers on your home network.

You will also learn how you can send media on your PC to DLNA-compatible devices on your home network using Windows 10, including how some third-party software can help you stream from your Windows 10 phone.

## Streaming from a DLNA Server to a Windows PC

When you have media servers set up on your network, you can stream media stored on them from a Windows 10 PC. In the previous chapter, you set up Windows Media Player to work as a DLNA server, and you can also use Windows Media Player to access content on other PCs on your network using DLNA.

Information

DLNA is a standard allowing media devices from different (or the same) manufacturers to share content over the network they are attached to. This standard is controlled by the Digital Living Network Alliance. Quite a few devices support DLNA, including many smart TVs and modern audio systems.

When you run Windows Media Player on a PC running on the same network as other PCs, you will see listed libraries that have been shared from the other PCs (Figure [5-1](#A978-1-4842-1473-2_5_Chapter.html#Fig1)).

![A978-1-4842-1473-2_5_Fig1_HTML.jpg](A978-1-4842-1473-2_5_Fig1_HTML.jpg)

Figure 5-1.

PCs will be shown if they have shared their libraries in Windows Media Player (as discussed in the previous chapter) or are using third-party DLNA software such as Emby, which is discussed in the next chapter.

As well as seeing PCs with Windows Media Player (also known as WMP) running in shared mode, you will see other DLNA servers on your network, which could include NAS devices, Sonos devices, and other media servers.

Information

DLNA is based on another standard: Universal Plug and Play Audio Visual (UPnP AV). Some manufacturers produce UPnP devices that will fully comply with the DLNA standard but do not want to call them DLNA because there is a licensing cost involved. This means that while not guaranteed, many UPnP Av devices will work perfectly happily with Windows 10’s DLNA features.

If you don’t see any shared libraries, then either the other PCs are not configured correctly or the network you are connected to is currently in Public mode, which means it will not browse and connect to other devices. See the previous chapter for how to change your network to Private mode.

# 6. Setting Up Media Servers to Stream Your Content

So far in this book you have looked at media streaming around your home network and from the cloud, but what if you have your own music and video collection and want to be able to access it from anywhere around the world? ## Plex Media Server

Plex is a media server that is designed to be a simple solution for storing and organizing all your personal media collection and for serving it to Plex clients on your various devices. You install Plex Media Server on a PC, where your music and videos collection is located, and then you can access your collection from various devices including Windows PCs, Windows phones, and iOS and Android devices. Plex is a powerful and flexible media solution, and in this section you will set up a basic server and connect to it via Windows 10.

The first step to getting started with Plex is to go to Plex.tv from your web browser and create a user account. You will need an account so that your Plex Media Server and Plex app on Windows 10 can talk to each other.

Information

Once you have created a Plex user account, you will need to download and install the Plex Media Server software.

Note

The Plex Media Server software needs to be installed on your PC that has your media located on it, so from that PC’s web browser, go to [`http://Plex.TV/downloads`](http://plex.tv/downloads) and download the Windows version.

Once the download has completed, run the program, and it will launch the Plex Media Server Setup.

Click Install to start the setup. Windows User Account Control will ask you to confirm that the setup can make changes to your system; click Yes to continue with the install.

When the installer has completed, click Launch to start configuring Plex.

First you must agree to the terms of service (probably a good idea to read them as well!). When you have read them, click the Agree button at the bottom of the terms box.

Now you need to tell the server the Plex account you created earlier and then configure you media locations. So, click the Settings icon on the left side and select Settings from the menu (Figure [6-1](#A978-1-4842-1473-2_6_Chapter.html#Fig1)).

## Resources for Configuring Media Servers on Other Platforms

In this chapter, you looked at Plex Media Server and Emby Server, which are powerful media servers that also have client apps for many different platforms including Windows 10\. In this section, you will take a brief look at two other media servers that do not have specific Windows 10 clients but will run on Windows 10 and a lot of other platforms. They are definitely worth a look. These are Kodi and MediaPortal.

### Kodi

Kodi is an open source media server project that was previously known as Xbox Media Center. It started life as a media solution for the original Xbox and has become a popular system. Kodi can be installed on a wide range of platforms including Windows 10 and has media management features, network streaming, and community add-ins. To find out more, go to [`http://Kodi.tv`](http://kodi.tv/) where you will be able to read about its features, and you will find the download links.

### Media Portal

MediaPortal is a media solution designed for a PC. Unlike Plex and Emby, which are client-server systems, MediaPortal is an all-in-one media system that you can connect to a big-screen TV or projector. It supports live TV, recorded TV, music, and movie playback, and it has a wide range of plug-ins available. Go to [`www.team-mediaportal.com`](http://www.team-mediaportal.com/) for more details and the download links.
